# Ultra-strong and ultra-tough hydrogels through multi-scale hierarchical structuring

> **来源**: [nature.com](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-48017-9)
> **剪藏日期**: 2026-04-30

## 摘要

Hydrogels are promising materials for various applications, but their mechanical performance is often limited by the trade-off between strength and toughness. Here we report a strategy to create ultra-strong and ultra-tough hydrogels by introducing multi-scale hierarchical structures. The hydrogels exhibit a tensile strength of 12.5 MPa and fracture toughness of 45,000 J/m², outperforming most existing hydrogels. The hierarchical design includes molecular-level crosslinking, nanofibril reinforcement, and micro-scale porosity, enabling efficient energy dissipation across multiple length scales.

## 全文

The development of hydrogels with both high strength and high toughness has been a long-standing challenge. Traditional approaches such as double-network hydrogels or nanocomposite hydrogels have improved mechanical properties but still face limitations. Here we demonstrate a bioinspired approach that mimics the hierarchical structure of natural tissues.

Our method involves three key steps: (1) formation of a chemically crosslinked polymer network, (2) in situ growth of cellulose nanofibrils within the network, and (3) freeze-casting to create anisotropic microporous structures. The resulting hydrogels show exceptional mechanical properties with a tensile strength of 12.5 ± 0.8 MPa, elastic modulus of 8.2 ± 0.5 MPa, and fracture toughness of 45,000 ± 3,000 J/m².

Cyclic loading-unloading tests reveal significant hysteresis, indicating efficient energy dissipation through the hierarchical structure. The hydrogels also demonstrate excellent fatigue resistance, maintaining 85% of their original strength after 10,000 loading cycles.

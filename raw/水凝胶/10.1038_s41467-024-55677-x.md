# Hierarchically aligned heterogeneous core-sheath hydrogels

> **来源**: [Nature Communications](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-55677-x)
> **剪藏日期**: 2026-04-30

## Authors
Zhao Xu, Hong Chen, Huai-Bin Yang, Xin Yao, Haili Qin, Huai-Ping Cong*, Shu-Hong Yu*

## 摘要

Natural load-bearing tissues such as tendons and ligaments exhibit exceptional mechanical properties due to their hierarchically aligned heterogeneous structures. Inspired by this, we develop a strategy to fabricate hierarchically aligned heterogeneous core-sheath hydrogels through a self-assembly assisted salting-out process. The resulting hydrogels feature a compactly crosslinked sheath and an aligned porous core with nanofibril alignment at multiple scales. These hydrogels achieve ultrahigh mechanical properties: toughness of 1031 MJ m⁻³, strength of 55.3 MPa, strain of 3300%, stiffness of 6.8 MPa, fracture energy of 552.7 kJ m⁻², and a fatigue threshold of 40.9 kJ m⁻², outperforming most previously reported hydrogels. The hierarchical design provides efficient energy dissipation and crack resistance, making these hydrogels promising for load-bearing applications.

### Introduction
Hydrogels are promising for various applications but typically suffer from poor mechanical properties. Natural tissues achieve remarkable performance through hierarchical structures spanning multiple length scales, from molecular to macroscopic levels. Recapitulating such hierarchical design in synthetic hydrogels remains challenging.

### Results
The fabrication involves a self-assembly assisted salting-out process. Polyvinyl alcohol (PVA) solutions undergo freeze-thaw cycling to form crystalline domains, followed by immersion in a sodium citrate solution to induce salting-out. This creates a core-sheath structure with a dense, highly crosslinked sheath (≈200 μm thick) and a porous, aligned core.

The hierarchical structure was characterized by SEM, TEM, and SAXS. The sheath shows compactly packed nanofibrils with diameter of 20-50 nm, while the core exhibits aligned porous channels with nanofibril bundles oriented along the stretching direction.

Mechanical testing revealed:
- Tensile strength: 55.3 ± 2.1 MPa
- Elongation at break: 3300 ± 150%
- Young's modulus: 6.8 ± 0.3 MPa
- Toughness: 1031 ± 45 MJ m⁻³
- Fracture energy: 552.7 ± 20.1 kJ m⁻²
- Fatigue threshold: 40.9 ± 1.8 kJ m⁻²

Cyclic testing showed stable regeneration over 10 cycles with minimal hysteresis decay.

## 4.4 Environmental -- Heavy Metal Adsorption (CMC/modified cellulose hydrogel, cold effluent)

### Recommended paper
**Title:** Removal of Heavy Metal Ions by Poly(vinyl alcohol) and Carboxymethyl Cellulose Composite Hydrogels Prepared by a Freeze-Thaw Method
**Authors:** Wang L.Y., Wang M.J.
**Journal:** ACS Sustainable Chemistry & Engineering, 2016, 4(5), 2830-2837
**DOI:** 10.1021/acssuschemeng.6b00336
**Key results:** PVA/CMC hydrogels prepared by freeze-thaw (cold temperature fabrication). Adsorption of Ag+, Ni2+, Cu2+, Zn2+ via chelation. The freeze-thaw preparation method inherently demonstrates structural integrity at low temperatures. Metal ions reduced on hydrogels.
**Confidence:** HIGH (freeze-thaw processing implies cold stability)

### Alternative (temperature-dependent adsorption study)
**Title:** Functionalized Cyclodextrin/Carboxymethyl Cellulose Composite Hydrogel with Double Network Structure for Adsorption of Heavy Metal Ions in Wastewater
**Authors:** Zhang H. et al.
**Journal:** Molecules, 2024, 29(22), 5414
**DOI:** 10.3390/molecules29225414
**Key results:** Temperature effect studied (25-65 deg C). Adsorption DECREASED with increasing temperature (exothermic), meaning lower/cold temperatures enhance adsorption. Cu2+: 158.12 to 74.31 mg/g; Pb2+: 393.56 to 320.17 mg/g; Cd2+: 290.12 to 217.18 mg/g as temp rose from 25 to 65 deg C.
**Confidence:** HIGH (directly demonstrates cold-temperature adsorption advantage)

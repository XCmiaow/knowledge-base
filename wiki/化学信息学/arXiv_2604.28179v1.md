Bronchoscopic navigation relies on registering endoscopic video to a preoperative CT scan, but respiratory motion deforms the airway by 5-20 mm, creating CT-to-body divergence that limits localization accuracy. In practice, this is mitigated through breath-hold protocols, which attempt to match the intraoperative anatomy to a static CT, but are difficult to reproduce and disrupt clinical workflow.

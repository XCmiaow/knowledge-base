presented WCA of about 85° when drop was deposited,
while those prepared with CNF-E had about 10° less.
These values are significantly high compared to those
reported in the literature, where WCA are usually in the
range of 50°-55° (Sun et al. 2015). The high density of
surface hydroxyl groups both in cellulose and hemi-
cellulose confer to CNF a huge hydrophilic character
and, according to Dankovich and Gray (2011), WCA
lower than 40° is expected. The higher values obtained
in this work may be explained by two effects: (1) the
presence of residual lignin in the form of a thin layer
blocking part of the surface hydroxyl groups (Ferrer
et al. 2012) and/or (2) the presence of residual
extractives. Besides, after 30 s of the drop deposition,
WCAs were not excessively decreased, fact that might
be explained by the low porosity of the nanopapers,
hindering water absorption by capillarity.

# Cellulose nanofibers/liquid metal hydrogels with high tensile strength, environmental adaptability and electromagnetic shielding for temperature monitoring and strain sensors

## 摘要

Hydrogel sensors are important in flexible electronics and human motion monitoring, but it remains challenging to integrate multiple excellent properties and ensure compatibility between conductive fillers and hydrogel matrices. In this work, liquid metal droplets stabilized by cellulose nanofibers were used to initiate polymerization of acrylamide monomer, while reduced graphene oxide bridged the liquid metal droplets. After further crosslinking in glycerol, the resulting CNF@LM/polyacrylamide/rGO/gelatin/glycerol hydrogel showed tensile strain above 1317%, environmental adaptability from −80 to 80 °C, adhesion, high sensitivity in both strain and temperature sensing, as well as electromagnetic shielding and antimicrobial activity.

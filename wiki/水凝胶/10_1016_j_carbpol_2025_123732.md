# Nanocellulose-mediated double-network conductive organohydrogels with self-adhesive, biocompatible and environmentally tolerant properties for wearable biosensors

## 摘要

Conductive hydrogels are promising because of stretchability, self-adhesion and biocompatibility, but they often freeze at low temperature and dry at high temperature. This work reports a highly stretchable, self-adhesive, biocompatible, extremely temperature-tolerant double-network conductive organohydrogel based on TEMPO-oxidized cellulose nanofibers-graphene/polyacrylamide-polyacrylic acid-polydopamine-glycerol (TG/P-LP-Gl). Strong hydrogen bonding between glycerol and water inhibits icing and evaporation. With 50% glycerol, the organohydrogel reaches tensile strength of 110.3 kPa, compressive strength of 2.64 MPa, adhesive strength of 29.7 kPa, retains performance at −20 °C and 70 °C, and functions as a wearable strain sensor with a gauge factor around 15.

# A robust, biodegradable and recyclable all-cellulose ionogel from low-value wood

## 摘要

For thousands of years, wood has been utilized as a natural, abundant, and sustainable material for structural construction and furniture. To enhance its value, the authors developed a simple process that directly converts low-value wood, including cracked wood, chips, shives, and other residues, into high-performance all-cellulose ionogels. The process involves delignification, in situ dissolution of cellulose, and self-assembly of molecular cellulose chains. The resulting ionogel shows high ionic conductivity of approximately 60 mS cm−1, mechanical strength of approximately 6 MPa, self-healing within about 10 min, and freeze tolerance down to −50 °C while retaining conductivity around 6.4 mS cm−1. It is further demonstrated as a flexible circuit substrate, a breathable multimodal sensor, and a low-temperature supercapacitor electrolyte, and the all-cellulose framework naturally degrades in moist soil within 15 days while the ionic liquid can be reused.

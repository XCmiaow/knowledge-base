# A self-healing stretchable hydrogel sensor for hand movement disorder monitoring

## 摘要

> The key to accurately quantifying hand movement disorders lies in the support of high-performance sensors. Existing sensing technologies struggle to simultaneously meet the dual demands of high sensitivity and a wide strain range, and they often cannot recover after mechanical damage. To address this, the paper proposes a self-healing stretchable hydrogel sensor based on acrylamide glycinamide (NAGA) and acrylamide (AAm). Within 0-100% strain, the sensor shows excellent sensitivity, high linearity, low hysteresis error, and low repeatability error. After autonomous healing at 50 °C for 20 minutes, the self-healing efficiency reaches 84.8% without significant performance loss. When attached to hand joints, it can distinguish different amplitudes and frequencies of motion, including low-frequency micro-tremors between 0.2 and 2 Hz, indicating potential for intelligent medical care and personalized diagnosis.
